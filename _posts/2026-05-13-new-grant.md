### Dr. Hridesh Rajan Awarded NSF Grant

Dr. Hridesh Rajan, Dean of the School of Science and Engineering at Tulane University and Professor of Computer Science, has been awarded a National Science Foundation grant for his project, "Collaborative Research: SHF: Small: Fault Localization for Deep Learning."

The project is a collaborative effort between Tulane University and Oakland University. The project will be led at Tulane by Dean Dr. Rajan, collaborating with Dr. Mohammad Wardat from Oakland University. NSF will provide $376,008 to Tulane University in support of research on techniques for identifying and localizing faults in deep learning applications. This funding falls under Computer and Information Science and Engineering (CISE): Core Programs.
